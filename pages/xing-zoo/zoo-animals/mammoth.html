Woolly Mammoth

Grazing the icy tundras of the Northern Hemisphere, the woolly mammoth is a majestic, prehistoric relative of the modern elephant. Recognized by its thick, shaggy hair, curved tusks that can span several meters, and a pronounced hump of fat behind its head, this herbivore is supremely adapted to cold environments. A staple in the Ice Age ecosystems, it interacts with other megafauna, playing a vital role in shaping its environment.
